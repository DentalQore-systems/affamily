The foundational principles of oral hygiene–brushing, flossing, and rinsing regularly–will not achieve exact results for every patient. The quality of your dental health also depends on the oral hygiene products you use, as they are not all equal. The ADA Seal of Acceptance helps you to choose high-quality oral care products for your smile.<\/span>
\n<\/span>
\n<\/span>While few dental products are actually a danger to your smile, many only offer cosmetic value despite their marketing claims. If you are looking for hygiene products that live up to their packaging, choose products that have the ADA Seal of Acceptance. Introduced in 1931, this program is intended to help you identify oral hygiene products that are good quality, safe, and meet their marketing claims.<\/span>
\n<\/span>
\n<\/span>To receive the ADA Seal of Acceptance, a product is submitted to the ADA and its active ingredients undergo rigorous testing for safety and efficacy under strict guidelines. The presence of the Seal of Acceptance on a label proves that a product is safe for use and meets its oral health claims.<\/span>
