Having sensitive teeth can be extremely inconvenient and painful, especially when eating and drinking your favorite foods and drinks, but there are many things you can do to change that, and we are happy to tell you all about them!<\/p>\n

If you do the following, you will have a good chance of soothing your tooth sensitivity:<\/p>\n

\u2022\tBrush your teeth twice a day.
\n\u2022\tFloss your teeth once a day.
\n\u2022\tRinse your mouth with fluoride mouthwash.
\n\u2022\tBrush your teeth with a soft-bristled toothbrush and toothpaste that caters to sensitivity.
\n\u2022\tWatch what you eat and try to avoid eating acidic foods.
\n\u2022\tIf you have a teeth-grinding habit, protect your teeth with a mouth guard.
\n\u2022\tVisit your dentist every six months.<\/p>\n

These remedies can help with the many dental problems that can cause tooth sensitivity. To identify the cause of your tooth sensitivity, you can look for one or more of the following:<\/p>\n

\u2022\tGums moving away from your teeth, exposing the gums
\n\u2022\tTooth decay near the gum line
\n\u2022\tThe symptoms of gum disease
\n\u2022\tPlaque buildup
\n\u2022\tTooth wear and tear
\n\u2022\tTooth damage<\/p>\n

If you would like a more specific diagnosis or if you would like information on the remedy that will help you the most, call our office today and schedule an appointment.
